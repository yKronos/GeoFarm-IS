# Objectives of the Study

This study aims to develop a web-based Agricultural Management System with GIS and Predictive Farmer Analytics (GeoFarm-IS) that digitizes farmer registration, enables geospatial monitoring of agricultural activities, and provides predictive analytics to identify farmers at risk of financial loss for the LGU Agriculture Office of Tumauini, Isabela.

Specifically, this study seeks to:

1. Determine the problems involved in the existing manual farmer registration and data management system of the Municipal Agriculture Office of Tumauini, Isabela.

2. Develop a web-based Agricultural Management System (GeoFarm-IS) that contains the following modules:
   a. Centralized Farmer Registry with comprehensive farmer profiles (personal information, landholdings, crops per season, livestock inventory) and QR code generation for faster transactions;
   b. Seasonal Agricultural Tracking module that monitors farmer activities, crop production volumes, and inputs across dry and wet seasons;
   c. Geographic Information System (GIS) Mapping module that visualizes farmer land locations, crop distribution, and livestock density across the municipality;
   d. Predictive Farmer Analytics module that identifies farmers on track to experience losses ("palugi") based on seasonal data, enabling early intervention; and
   e. Financial Assistance and Livestock Tracking module that monitors aid distribution and livestock inventory for each farmer.

3. Test the developed system using:
   a. Alpha testing—conducted by the development team to identify and resolve technical defects; and
   b. Beta testing—conducted by selected Agriculture Office staff and farmers in a real-world operational environment.

4. Evaluate the developed system using the ISO 25010 quality standard in terms of:
   a. Functional Suitability—completeness and correctness of system features;
   b. Performance Efficiency—system response time and resource utilization during normal and peak operations;
   c. Compatibility—ability to operate across different browsers, devices, and operating systems;
   d. Usability—ease of use, navigation, and user satisfaction for staff and farmers;
   e. Reliability—system stability, consistency, and error-handling capabilities during peak usage periods;
   f. Security—protection of farmer data through authentication, authorization, and data encryption;
   g. Maintainability—ease of system updates, modifications, and debugging; and
   h. Portability—adaptability for deployment across different hardware and software environments.
