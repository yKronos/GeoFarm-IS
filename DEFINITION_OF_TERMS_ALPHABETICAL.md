# Definition of Terms

The following terms are defined conceptually and operationally to ensure clarity and better understanding of this study:

**Agile SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle)** – A software development methodology characterized by iterative development cycles (sprints), continuous stakeholder feedback, and incremental delivery of functional software components. In this study, the Scrum framework under Agile SDLC guides the development of GeoFarm-IS.

**Geographic Information System (GIS)** – Conceptually, a GIS is a computer-based system for capturing, storing, checking, and displaying data related to positions on the earth's surface (Longley et al., 2022). Operationally, in this study, GIS refers to the interactive mapping module of GeoFarm-IS built using Leaflet.js and PostGIS that visualizes farmer land locations, crop distribution, and livestock density.

**GeoFarm-IS** – Stands for Geographic Farm Information System. In this study, it refers to the web-based Agricultural Management System with GIS and Predictive Farmer Analytics developed for the LGU Agriculture Office of Tumauini, Isabela.

**ISO 25010** – An international standard for systems and software quality models. In this study, it serves as the evaluation framework for assessing GeoFarm-IS in terms of Functional Suitability, Performance Efficiency, Compatibility, Usability, Reliability, Security, Maintainability, and Portability.

**Palugi** – A Filipino/Ilocano colloquial term meaning "to incur a loss" or "to operate at a loss." In this study, it refers specifically to farmers who experience financial losses from agricultural activities within a given season, as identified by the GeoFarm-IS predictive analytics module.

**PostGIS** – A spatial database extender for PostgreSQL and MySQL that allows geospatial data types such as point coordinates and polygons to be stored, queried, and analyzed. In this study, PostGIS is used to store farmer landholding coordinates for GIS map rendering.

**Predictive Analytics** – Conceptually, predictive analytics encompasses statistical and machine learning techniques that analyze current and historical data to make forecasts about future events (World Bank, 2022). Operationally, in this study, it refers to the rule-based and statistical algorithm within GeoFarm-IS that identifies farmers at risk of financial losses ("palugi") based on their seasonal production data.

**QR Code (Quick Response Code)** – A type of matrix barcode that can be scanned using a smartphone or scanner to quickly access encoded digital information. In this study, QR codes are generated for each registered farmer and printed on ID cards to enable fast, paperless transactions at the Agriculture Office.

**RSBSA (Registry System for Basic Sectors in Agriculture)** – A national farmer registration database mandated by the Department of Agriculture to establish a comprehensive registry of farmers and fisherfolk in the Philippines. GeoFarm-IS is designed to be compliant with RSBSA data requirements.

**Seasonal Agricultural Tracking** – In this study, this refers to the module within GeoFarm-IS that records and monitors farmer agricultural activities, crop production volumes, input costs, and yields across the dry season (November–April) and wet season (May–October).
